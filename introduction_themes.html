Every web site needs a theme - a single brand, author, style or organisation for example - because unlike examples of other media, there is no more tangible physical form to separate one from another. Hilton Harbour has several themes, some obvious and some more tenuous...

Computers: articles like How to choose computer books, which was featured in an interview with Peter Hilton on CJOB 68 radio in March 2005.

Cafes: Amsterdam, Cambridge (also restaurants), Florence, Galway, Leuven, London, Luxembourg, Maastricht, Madrid, Oxford, Paris, Rotterdam and Vienna have good cafes that I have written about. European Cafe Watch keeps track of the best, never forgetting that every cafe needs a sofa.

Travel: cafes aside, I have also written about Bordeaux, Budapest, Luxembourg, Padstow and Vienna. On the way I compiled the European Phrase Book.

The design: Hilton Harbour, to some degree, is a personal experiment in designing and implementing a web site. This is captured by my Web Site Architecture guidelines and the Web Page Design Principles.

An author: Peter Hilton, created most of the pages and photos; but Marion wrote about working in England, my father wrote the poetry, his father wrote about Yorkshire and Dunkirk, and his father (my great-grandfather) wrote about the theatre.

Photos: try the photo index and the small collections for Antwerp, Budapest, Cafes On The Inside, Cafes On The Outside, Edinburgh, City of Luxembourg, Maastricht, transport, Vienna.

More: contents, index and what's new pages, as well as the other introduction.
